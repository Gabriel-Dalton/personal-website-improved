About

I was born on a boat and lived there for six years.

At 10 years old, I taught myself to code, and by 15, I launched Oasis of Change, Inc., a not-for-profit focused on digital sustainability — building energy-efficient websites that help organizations grow while using less power.

Since then, I’ve helped plant over 9,000 trees in 20 countries, prevented more than 7,000 plastic bottles from entering the ocean, and supported nonprofits in securing up to $868,200 per year in in-kind technology grants.

I’ve presented to audiences of hundreds, including the TransLink Executive Team and Board of Directors, and the Vancouver Emerging Technology Showcase. My work has been recognized with the Impact in Innovation Award from the City of Vancouver.
